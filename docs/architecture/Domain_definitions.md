## Sign Convention of Power and Energy values

Energy (W_imp) that is imported from utilities has positive value.
Energy (W_exp) that is exported to utilities has negative value.

There is only one Energy flow to/from utilitis. Only one value, either pos or neg. 
    W_util = W_imp + W_exp

But two different tariffs which apply depending on the direction/sign.


Energy (W_con) consumed by users asset has positive value.
Energy (W_gen) generated by users asset has negative value.
Energy (W_sto) that gets stored has pos value (it's consumption that can be released later).
Energy (W_rel) that gets released (from storge, mostly batteries) has neg value.


*Energy (Work) is Integral of Power over time:*

    W = Integral[P(t) × dt]


*Price (or GHG Value) is Integral of Power multiplied tariff(depending on direction and time, tariffs are changing over time) over time: (Utilities separate prices of Inport and Export on utility bill but in VEN, we accumulate it)*

    Price of Utilitie Energy = Integral[P_util(t) × tariff(t,sign(P_util(t))) × dt]

    Units Equation:  [€] = [kW] × [€/kWh] × [h]
    Units Equation:  [GHG] = [kW] × [GHG/kWh] × [h]


*Tariffs for import and export are both positive so that they can be multiplied with the signed power to result in a true price that costs the user if price is positive or benefits the user if price is negative*

*At any time is true (where P_imp OR P_exp = 0 [W]):*

    Σ(P)  =  P_imp + P_exp - (P_con + P_gen + P_sto + P_rel)  =  0 [W]
    Σ(P)  =  P_util - (P_con + P_gen + P_sto + P_rel)  =  0 [W]

    In other words, the sum of all power flows that are connected to the central connection board is always 0 W (no energy generated or stored in the central connection board).

    It can be confusing to see that consumed and generated power is added but it makes sense if one is aware that they are values with predefined signs (which can not change).
